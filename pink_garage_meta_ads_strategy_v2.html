01 — Brand & Location Context

Understanding Where You're Selling

The Pink Garage by Sadhvi is a new boutique operating out of Whitefield, Bengaluru, selling Indian suits, sarees, and co-ords. This product mix is strategically significant — it means the brand serves a wider age range and occasion spectrum than a single-category boutique, which directly shapes how we build audiences and write ads.

Whitefield is East Bengaluru's largest IT and residential hub. It is home to major tech parks (ITPL, Prestige Tech Park, RMZ Ecoworld), thousands of residential apartment complexes, and one of the most densely packed working-professional populations in India. This makes it a cosmopolitan, aspirational, digitally active neighbourhood — not a traditional ethnic fashion market.

What this means for your ads: Your audience is not a homogenous local crowd. Whitefield is made up of South Indian professionals (for whom sarees are deeply cultural), North Indian migrants (who specifically seek suits and occasion wear), and younger IT professionals (who are drawn to co-ords as everyday ethnic-fusion wear). All three are reachable on Meta — but each needs a different message.

This is both an opportunity and a challenge. The opportunity: a curated boutique offering suits, sarees, and co-ords under one roof in Whitefield is genuinely differentiated. Most large chains (Fab India, Biba, W, Nalli) are either in malls or specialize narrowly. The challenge: Bengaluru is a Tier-1 metro — ad costs are higher and competition for screen space is fierce.

02 — Perception Analysis

How Whitefield Women See You on Meta

Before spending a rupee on ads, we must understand what happens in a Bengaluru woman's mind the moment The Pink Garage appears in her Instagram feed. Perception controls everything that follows.

👁️

First Impression in 1.5 Seconds

The name "Pink Garage" is distinctive — it signals personality, femininity, and a non-corporate vibe. A Whitefield professional scrolling Instagram will clock it as something independent and curated. That's valuable brand equity before a single product is shown.

💭

The Whitefield Audience Mindset

These are working women aged 24–45, predominantly salaried professionals or entrepreneurs. They are time-poor and visually literate. They don't want to spend a Sunday afternoon hunting through mall chain stores. A boutique that comes to them through Instagram — with clear, beautiful product presentation — solves a real problem.

🌍

Three Products = Three Audiences

Suits attract the broadest cross-community buyer. Sarees pull in South Indian women (for whom they're cultural, not occasional) and North Indian diaspora dressing for weddings and festivals. Co-ords attract a younger, fashion-forward crowd who follow trends and discover brands through Reels. Each audience needs a distinct ad angle — running one generic ad for all three products dilutes the message for everyone.

❤️

Emotional Trigger

Indian ethnic wear is deeply emotional — connected to identity, celebration, and self-expression. In Bengaluru's multicultural context, a well-curated Indian suit carries the added weight of cultural belonging for women far from home. Ads that acknowledge this — subtly — will outperform product-only content.

⚠️

Primary Concern: Trust

As a new, unknown brand, the single biggest barrier is trust. Customers will wonder: "Is this quality real? How does the fabric feel? Will it look as good in person?" The strategy must aggressively address this through social proof, behind-the-scenes content, and founder visibility — before asking for a walk-in.

📍

Walk-in Friction is Real

Whitefield residents are busy and traffic-averse. Convincing someone to physically travel to a new boutique requires strong desire or a compelling reason. The ad strategy must build that desire at TOFU, address questions at MOFU, and then give a clear, low-friction reason to visit at BOFU — not the reverse.

04 — Campaign Funnel

The Three-Stage Funnel

For a brand-new boutique, the funnel cannot be skipped or collapsed. You cannot run conversion ads to people who have never heard of you. Each stage serves a purpose in sequence.

Top of Funnel — TOFU

Awareness & Discovery

Reel ads · Stories · Instagram Feed · Objective: Reach / Video Views
Goal: Make Whitefield women stop, watch, and remember the brand name. No hard sell. Build familiarity.

Middle of Funnel — MOFU

Consideration & Engagement

Carousel ads · Collection ads · Messages objective · Instagram DM
Goal: Turn interest into intent. Show multiple products, invite questions, start conversations.

Bottom of Funnel — BOFU

Conversion — Walk-in & Lead

WhatsApp Click-to-Chat · Lead Form · Get Directions / Store Traffic objective
Goal: Give warm audiences a clear, low-friction path to physically visit the boutique.

WhatsApp Click-to-Chat

One-tap opens a WhatsApp conversation. Pre-filled: "Hi, I saw your ad on Instagram. I'd love to visit the boutique — can you share your address?" Highest walk-in conversion rate for local boutiques.

Priority #2

Carousel Ads

Show 5–8 suits in one ad. Simulates browsing in-store. Each card can feature a different outfit with a brief description. Drives multiple touches per impression.

Priority #3

Collects name + phone number directly on Meta. No landing page needed. Use to build a local database for WhatsApp follow-ups, new collection alerts, and exclusive previews.

Medium Priority

Meta's dedicated objective for driving physical visits. Shows map pin, store hours, call button, and "Get Directions" CTA. Targets women within a defined km radius of the boutique.

Creative Production Guidelines

  • Sadhvi must appear on camera. A founder-led boutique brand in a cosmopolitan city builds trust faster when the face behind it is visible. Even one 20-second Reel of Sadhvi introducing a new piece will outperform any product-only visual.
  • Film in the actual boutique space. Real environment, real product, real founder — this is the antidote to the "is this legitimate?" concern that kills conversions for new brands.
  • Prioritise natural light and clean backgrounds that match the pink/rose brand palette. Avoid heavy studio setups — the boutique's own ambiance is the best set you have.
  • Show the garment on a real person, not a mannequin or flat-lay. Bengaluru's fashion-savvy audience needs to see how fabric moves, how a collar sits, how a dupatta drapes.
  • Festival calendar content is non-negotiable. Plan creative 3–4 weeks ahead for: Navratri, Dasara (big in Karnataka), Diwali, Karva Chauth, Eid, Christmas party season, Ugadi. Ethnic wear demand spikes reliably around every one of these.
  • Customer content is gold. If a customer is happy to be photographed in their purchase inside the boutique, that UGC (user-generated content) becomes the most trusted ad format you can run.
  • Language: Primary copy in English. Optional Hindi copy for North Indian diaspora ad sets. Do not use Punjabi-specific language or regional references that don't apply to a Bengaluru audience.
07 — Campaign Structure

Phase 1 — 30-Day Campaign Blueprint

Three campaigns run simultaneously from launch day. Importantly, with three distinct product lines, each campaign now has product-specific ad sets so messaging stays targeted and relevant. Running one generic ad for suits, sarees, and co-ords together dilutes all three.

Campaign 01

Brand Awareness

  • Objective: Video Views / Reach
  • Format: Reels + Stories
  • Audience: Cold — interest-based, 8km radius
  • Budget: 40% of monthly spend
  • Ad Sets: 3 — one per product line (suits / sarees / co-ords), each with age-appropriate targeting
  • Creatives: 1 Reel per product line minimum (3 Reels total to start)
Campaign 02

Messages & Engagement

  • Objective: Messages (WhatsApp)
  • Format: Carousel + Single image
  • Audience: Warm — Reel viewers 50%+, page engagers (last 60 days)
  • Budget: 35% of monthly spend
  • Ad Sets: 2 — broad warm pool + product-specific retargeting once data builds
  • Creatives: 1 carousel per product, rotating
Campaign 03

Walk-in Conversion

  • Objective: Leads / Store Traffic
  • Format: Lead Form + Get Directions
  • Audience: Hot — DM replied, post savers, lead form openers
  • Budget: 25% of monthly spend
  • Ad Sets: 1–2 high-intent segments
  • Creatives: 1 lead form ad (all products), 1 store traffic ad with location pin

07B — Budget Planning

The Complete Budget Breakdown

Sadhvi's confirmed budget is ₹10,000–₹20,000/month. This section is built specifically around that range — with an honest view of what it can and cannot achieve in Bengaluru's ad market, how to allocate every rupee across campaigns and product lines, and a clear roadmap for when and how to scale.

Bengaluru Reality Check: ₹10,000–₹20,000/month is a lean but workable budget for a Tier-1 metro — provided it is spent strategically. The biggest mistake at this budget level is spreading spend too thin across too many ad sets simultaneously. The rule here is: fewer ad sets, more focused spend, let the algorithm concentrate. Running 6 ad sets at ₹55/day each is less effective than running 3 ad sets at ₹110/day each.

08 — Pre-Launch Setup

What Must Be Ready Before the First Rupee is Spent

Many boutiques run ads and get zero results because the infrastructure behind the ad is broken. Fix these first:

  • WhatsApp Business account set up with a profile photo, store address, hours, and at least one quick-reply template for "I saw your Instagram ad." Slow replies kill conversions.
  • Instagram profile optimised: clear bio with location (Whitefield, Bengaluru), store hours, a link to Google Maps or a WhatsApp chat link. The profile is where people land from the ad — it must convert on its own.
  • Meta Business Suite + Ads Manager properly configured with a Business account (not a personal profile). Meta Pixel should be installed even if there's no website yet — at minimum, it can track Instagram profile visits.
  • A minimum of 9 pieces of content ready before ads go live: at least 3 short Reels (15–30 sec each), 3 product carousels, and 3 high-quality single-image posts. Running ads without content depth undermines the brand impression.
  • Google Business Profile claimed and verified for the boutique address in Whitefield. Meta's "Get Directions" ads work far better when the Google listing is live and accurate — and it drives organic local search traffic simultaneously.

10 — Risks & Mitigation

What Could Go Wrong & How We Win

🏙️
High CPM in Bengaluru Metro

Bengaluru is a Tier-1 city — ad costs are meaningfully higher than Tier-2. Under-budgeting will prevent the algorithm from leaving the learning phase. Mitigation: Start with a minimum of ₹500/day (₹15,000/month). Do not go below this. Prioritise Reels, which consistently deliver the lowest CPM in India.

🆕
Zero Brand Awareness (New Brand)

No one knows The Pink Garage yet. Mitigation: Spend the first 30 days building the retargeting pool. Do not try to run conversion ads to cold audiences. Sequence the funnel properly — awareness first, conversion later.

🏪
Competition from Organised Retail

Bengaluru has Fab India, W, Biba, SOCH, and dozens of Instagram boutiques all advertising. Mitigation: Do not compete on price or variety — compete on curation and founder personality. Sadhvi's personal story and specific aesthetic taste are things no chain can replicate.

🚦
Whitefield Traffic & Walk-in Reluctance

Bengaluru traffic is a genuine psychological barrier to visiting a new boutique. Mitigation: The BOFU ad must address this directly — clear parking information, easy location pin, "2 minutes from ITPL/Prestige" landmark references, and a compelling reason to visit (new collection, exclusive preview, limited pieces).

📸
Low Creative Quality

Poor lighting or blurry product images will damage brand perception instantly in a high-income, aesthetically-aware market like Whitefield. Mitigation: Invest in one half-day professional shoot before ads go live. The creative is not a cost — it is the ad itself.

💬
Slow DM/WhatsApp Response Time

If someone messages at 9pm after seeing an ad and gets a reply at 10am the next day — they have already moved on. Mitigation: Set up an automated WhatsApp Business greeting with store hours and address. Aim to reply personally within 1 hour during working hours.

11 — Client Summary

Sadhvi, Here Is Your 30-Day Roadmap

Plain Language — What We're Doing & Why

You're starting a boutique in Whitefield, one of Bengaluru's most active, high-income neighbourhoods. The goal is to build real local visibility and bring qualified women through your door. Here's the plan, week by week:

  • Before anything starts: Get WhatsApp Business set up, Instagram bio optimised with your Whitefield address, and at least 3 Reels filmed. Ads cannot fix a broken profile.
  • Week 1–2: Launch Reel ads targeting women within 8–10 km of your boutique who have shown interest in ethnic fashion. Pure visibility. No hard sell. Just make your name known.
  • Week 2–3: Launch WhatsApp Click-to-Chat ads retargeting anyone who watched your Reels or visited your profile. These women already know you — give them a one-tap way to start a conversation.
  • Week 3–4: Launch a Lead Form or Store Traffic ad to your warmest audience. Something that gives them a reason to visit: new collection just in, festival picks, or a simple "come see it in person" message with your exact location.
  • Monthly rhythm: Review what's working, pause what's not, refresh creatives monthly, and plan ahead for every festival on the calendar. Navratri, Dasara, Diwali, and wedding season are your biggest opportunities — start preparing 4 weeks early.
  • The real goal: By Month 3, you will have a warm Instagram audience in Whitefield that knows your name, trusts your taste, and thinks of The Pink Garage first when they need an Indian suit. That is the network you're building — one walk-in at a time.
